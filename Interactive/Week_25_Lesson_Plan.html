📌 CUE CARD: Describe a time when you encouraged someone to do something that he/she didn't want to do.

You should say: Who he or she is, What you encouraged him/her to do, How he/she reacted, And explain why you encouraged him/her to do it.

🏆 Band 6.5 Model Answer

To begin with, I want to talk about my best friend, who felt completely frustrated (沮丧的), and who was incredibly reluctant (不情愿的) to join a public speaking competition. Honestly, I knew this was an urgent (紧急的) opportunity for her growth. Therefore, I tried to be highly supportive (支持的) and persuasive (有说服力的). Basically, I managed to talk her into (说服) joining the contest. Furthermore, I reminded her that two heads are better than one (三个臭皮匠顶个诸葛亮 / 集思广益), offering to help her practice. Eventually, she decided to give it a shot (试一试). Ultimately, she realized it was a wise (明智的) decision. Overall, she was deeply appreciative (感激的) after winning third place.
💡 Analysis: Look for the Red sentence (3 clauses) and Blue words (Transitions).

Q4: What important decisions should be made by teenagers themselves?

Op Ideally, youths should choose their own hobbies, and they must select their own friends. Re Consequently, making these social choices helps them reduce parental dependency (依赖) and learn to handle peer pressure (同伴压力). Ex As a result, a teenager who picks their own sports club becomes much more independent.
  • Pick friends
  • Be free
  • Sports club
Your Bullet Point Notes:
📉 Band 5 Peer Check: Ask: 'Should teenagers pick their own clothes, or should parents decide?'
📈 Band 6 Peer Check: Ask: 'Does letting teenagers choose their friends help them grow up?'

Q5: Is positive reinforcement (encouragement) more effective than criticism?

Op Absolutely, praise builds trust, whereas harsh criticism (批评) often causes deep resentment. Re Actually, people are much more likely to improve their behavior when they receive gentle, constructive criticism (建设性批评) instead of anger. Ex For instance, praising a student's effort before correcting their math errors yields much better grades.
  • Yes better
  • Feel good
  • Help student
Your Bullet Point Notes:
📉 Band 5 Peer Check: Ask: 'Do you learn faster from praise or from being yelled at?'
📈 Band 6 Peer Check: Ask: 'Why is giving advice nicely better than giving angry criticism?'

Q6: How does peer pressure differ from positive encouragement?

Op Fundamentally, pressure forces conformity, while encouragement sparks genuine personal growth. Re Essentially, true mentorship (导师制度) lifts a person up, whereas toxic peer influence drags them down into bad habits. Ex To illustrate, forcing a friend to skip class is pressure, but studying together is pure encouragement.
  • Force vs help
  • Bad habits
  • Study together
Your Bullet Point Notes:
📉 Band 5 Peer Check: Ask: 'Is it good if your friends force you to skip homework?'
📈 Band 6 Peer Check: Ask: 'What is the difference between a good mentor and bad peer pressure?'
