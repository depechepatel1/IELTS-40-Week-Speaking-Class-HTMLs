Welcome to IELTS Speaking Mastery: An AI-Driven Approach

A Guide for Students and School Leaders
Teachers in Chinese classrooms often struggle to motivate students to spend enough time independently speaking English unless directly observed, severely limiting weekly speaking time. This 40-week IELTS Speaking preparation course is designed to solve this problem, guiding students from an IELTS Band 5.5 to a 6.5 or higher.
Researched and structured by the latest AI technologies, the curriculum integrates the most advanced global language education science with cutting-edge AI. It exceeds strict UK Qualified Teacher Status (QTS) standards by combining world-class classroom teaching with a revolutionary, "always-available" AI co-teacher, freeing human teachers to focus entirely on building confidence and deeper communication skills.

1. The Ultimate Advantage: 100% Real Exam Questions

Standard English textbooks take years to publish, meaning their practice questions are artificial and outdated by the time they reach the classroom. Because the real IELTS exam constantly rotates topics, this course abandons textbooks entirely.
Instead, every prompt students practice is an actual, recent IELTS speaking test question collected directly by language training centres across China (e.g., New Oriental). The curriculum is updated periodically as new questions are captured, ensuring students study active topics to face fewer surprises on test day.

2. The 24/7 AI Homework Teacher

To comply with student time constraints and eliminate passive worksheets, homework is a fast, highly effective spoken workout utilizing our custom Web App and cutting-edge Aliyun Qwen Audio AI.
  • Pre-Lesson (10 mins) - Structured Input (Shadow Reading): Students practice with an AI Speaking Avatar featuring a perfect UK or US accent. They listen to the next lesson's vocabulary and model answers, then repeat them instantly. This aligns with Stephen Krashen’s Input Hypothesis, ensuring students enter the classroom with the target language activated and their pronunciation muscles privately trained.
  • Post-Lesson (20 mins) - Retrieval Practice (AI Feedback): Students record unscripted answers to real exam questions into the app. The Qwen AI provides instant, 24/7 personalized feedback on grammar and fluency. This leverages Merrill Swain’s Output Hypothesis, proving that producing language forces learners to notice the gap between what they want to say and what they can say, triggering cognitive awareness that drives fluency.

3. The Foundation of Western Curriculum Design: Bloom's Taxonomy

Chinese students taking IELTS are preparing for Western universities, which expect analytical thinking rather than rote memorization. IELTS examiners actively penalize memorized scripts. This course utilizes the 2001 Anderson & Krathwohl revision of Bloom's Taxonomy to seamlessly transition students from rote memorization to complex, critical communication. This revision focuses on Action-Oriented Verbs (making "Creating" the highest level) and organizing knowledge into four dimensions: Factual, Conceptual, Procedural, and Metacognitive.
Mapping the Course to the Revised Taxonomy:
Thinking Process Knowledge Dimension IELTS Speaking Course Application
Remembering Factual Knowledge Recalling correct vocabulary and common idioms to talk about familiar topics (Parts 2 & 3).
Applying Procedural Knowledge Using the specific procedure of making quick, effective notes during the 1-minute Part 2 prep.
Analyzing Conceptual Knowledge Breaking down complex, abstract Part 3 questions to understand exactly what is being asked.
Evaluating Metacognitive Knowledge Judging one's own spoken practice recordings to detect inconsistencies, using AI feedback to measure progress.
Creating Procedural Knowledge Putting words, grammar, and ideas together to generate spontaneous, original spoken English under time pressure.

4. The Science of the Classroom

With the pre-lesson AI handling the input phase, 100% of the 80-minute lesson is preserved for interactive output and real-time human communication. Sitting at desks makes students sleepy and allows them to hide; instead, we utilize specific, well-researched methodologies to build real fluency:
  • "Thinking Classrooms" (Embodied Cognition): Students stand up and work in small groups on big wall whiteboards. Using the physical body wakes up the brain, and because markers erase easily, the fear of making permanent mistakes is eliminated.
  • The 5-3-2 Vocabulary Rule (Spaced Repetition): Every week, students learn exactly 5 topic words, 3 native idioms, and 2 recycled words from past weeks to lock English into long-term memory.
  • Idea Building (Schema Activation): To prevent students from freezing up, short videos provide background ideas. Students then draw visual mind maps on their whiteboards, building "Narrative Endurance"—the stamina to speak easily for 2 full minutes.
  • Harvard "Thinking Routines": For difficult IELTS Part 3 questions, we teach the O.R.E. formula (Opinion, Reason, Example), giving the brain a map to build deep, intelligent answers without stopping.
  • Instant 1-on-1 Coaching (Micro-Conferencing): Meeting UK QTS Standard 6 for formative assessment, the teacher constantly walks the room to give fast, 2-minute mini IELTS tests, fixing mistakes the exact moment they happen.

5. Differentiation & Scaffolding (Vygotsky)

Lessons operate within Lev Vygotsky’s Zone of Proximal Development (ZPD)—the space between what a student can do independently and what they can achieve with guidance.
  • Scaffolding: Weaker students receive heavy support via idea prompts and the O.R.E. logic templates to progress without frustration.
  • Differentiation: Stronger students are stretched through "Constraint-Based Challenges" (e.g., "Answer this question using a 3-clause sentence and one idiom").
By combining real collected exam questions, AI-driven course design, and the most advanced teaching science in the world today, this program is the ultimate IELTS preparation.

学生与学校领导者指南
中国课堂的教师们常常面临一个困境：除非在直接监督下，否则难以激励学生投入足够时间进行独立的英语口语练习，这严重限制了学生每周的口语训练时间。本套为期40周的雅思口语备考课程旨在解决这一问题，引导学生将雅思口语成绩从5.5分提升至6.5分或更高。
本课程体系基于最新AI技术进行研究和构建，将全球最先进的语言教育科学与尖端人工智能相融合。通过将世界一流的课堂教学与革命性的、"随时可用"的AI助教相结合，它超越了严格的英国合格教师身份（QTS）标准，使人类教师能够完全专注于培养学生的自信心和更深层次的沟通技能。

1. 终极优势：100%真实考题

标准英语教科书需要数年时间才能出版，这意味着当它们进入课堂时，其中的练习题往往是人为编造且过时的。由于真实的雅思考试会不断轮换话题，本课程完全摒弃了教科书。
取而代之的是，学生练习的每一个问题都是直接来自中国各地语言培训中心（如新东方）收集的、近期真实考过的雅思口语测试题。随着新问题的出现，课程内容会定期更新，确保学生学习的是当前活跃的话题，从而在考试日面对更少的意外。

2. 全天候AI作业辅导老师

为适应学生的时间限制并摒弃被动的练习题，本课程的作业是利用我们定制的Web应用程序和尖端的阿里云通义音频AI进行的快速、高效的"口语训练"。
  • 课前（10分钟）- 结构化输入（影子跟读）：学生与拥有完美英音或美音的AI口语虚拟形象进行练习。他们聆听下一节课的词汇和范文答案，然后立即进行复述模仿。这符合斯蒂芬·克拉申的"输入假说"，确保学生在进入课堂时，目标语言已被激活，发音肌肉也经过了私下训练。
  • 课后（20分钟）- 提取练习（AI反馈）：学生在应用程序中录制对真实考题的即兴回答。通义AI提供即时、全天候的个性化语法和流利度反馈。这利用了梅里尔·斯温的"输出假说"，实践证明，产出语言会迫使学习者注意到他们想说的和能说的之间的差距，从而激发促进流利度的认知意识。

3. 西方课程设计的基础：布鲁姆分类法

参加雅思考试的中国学生是为进入西方大学做准备，而西方大学期望的是分析性思维，而非死记硬背。雅思考官会主动惩罚背诵模板的考生。本课程运用2001年安德森和克拉斯沃尔修订的布鲁姆分类法，引导学生无缝地从机械记忆过渡到复杂的、批判性的沟通。此次修订侧重于以行动为导向的动词（将"创造"设为最高层级），并将知识组织为四个维度：事实性知识、概念性知识、程序性知识和元认知知识。

4. 课堂教学的科学

由于课前AI处理了"输入"环节，80分钟的课堂时间100%被保留用于互动式"输出"和实时人际沟通。坐在课桌前容易让学生犯困并隐藏自己；因此，我们采用了特定的、经过充分研究的方法来构建真正的流利度：
  • "思考型课堂"（体验式认知）：学生站立起来，在大白板上以小组形式合作。运用身体能唤醒大脑，而且由于马克笔笔迹易擦除，害怕犯下永久性错误的恐惧感也随之消除。
  • 5-3-2词汇法则（间隔重复）：每周，学生精确学习5个主题词汇、3个地道习语，并复习2个过去几周学过的词汇，从而将英语牢固地锁定在长期记忆中。
  • 观点构建（图式激活）：为防止学生"大脑空白"，通过短视频提供背景观点。然后学生在白板上绘制视觉思维导图，建立"叙事耐力"——即能轻松说满2分钟的持续表达能力。
  • 哈佛"思考常规"：针对棘手的雅思第3部分问题，我们教授O.R.E.公式（观点、理由、例子），为大脑构建深入、智慧应答的"思维地图"，避免卡顿。
  • 即时一对一辅导（微型会谈）：为满足英国QTS标准中关于形成性评估的第6条，教师会不断在教室中巡视，进行快速的、2分钟的迷你雅思测试，在学生犯错的那一刻立即纠正。

5. 差异教学与支架式教学（维果茨基）

课程的开展遵循利维·维果茨基的"最近发展区"（ZPD）理论——即学生独立完成任务的能力与其在指导下能够达到的水平之间的区域。
  • 支架式教学：通过观点提示和O.R.E.逻辑模板，为较弱的学生提供强力支持，使其能够在不受挫的情况下取得进步。
  • 差异教学：通过"基于约束的挑战"（例如："请用一个包含三个从句的句子和一个习语来回答这个问题"），向较强的学生提出更高要求，拓展其能力。
通过将真实收集的考题、AI驱动的课程设计以及当今世界最先进的教学科学相结合，本课程是终极的雅思备考方案。
