📌 CUE CARD: Describe a book you read that you found useful.

You should say: What it is, When you read it, Why you think it is useful, And explain how you felt about it.

🏆 Band 6.5 Model Answer

To begin with, I will talk about an educational (教育意义的) book I read, because it was extremely useful for my studies. Actually, it is a famous classic (经典的) novel that is currently very trendy (时髦的). Although many people heavily judge a book by its cover (以貌取人), the deep storyline here is incredibly captivating (迷人的). I think, the detailed chapters are highly thought-provoking (发人深省的) and entirely memorable (难忘的). For example, it was such an amazing page-turner (引人入胜的书) that I managed to completely lose oneself in (沉迷于...) it. To be honest, reading it on an loudly overcrowded (拥挤的) train helped me significantly.
💡 Analysis: Look for the Red sentence (3 clauses) and Blue words (Transitions).

Q1: What should the government do to make libraries better?

Op Generally speaking, the government should heavily fund public libraries, because improving local Accessibility (可访问性 / 便捷性) boosts national education. Re This is largely due to the fact that, providing free resources significantly increases basic Literacy (读写能力 / 素养) rates among poor families. Ex For example, when a dedicated Bookworm (书呆子 / 爱书的人) has free digital tablets, they can easily study anytime.
  • Add modern computers
  • Free internet access
  • Poorer students study

Q2: Which one is better, paper books or e-books?

Op In my view, e-books are much better, as they offer incredible convenience for modern daily travel. Re The primary reason is that, carrying heavy physical books is exhausting, whereas a digital device holds thousands of novels. Ex However, some traditional readers strongly prefer paper books because they actively resist modern digital Consumerism (消费主义) and screen glare.
  • Digital is lighter
  • Carries many books
  • Reading on trains
Write your answer (Use O.R.E.):
📉 Band 5 Peer Check: Ask: 'Do you like the feeling of holding a real book?'
📈 Band 6 Peer Check: Ask: 'Why do some teachers think paper books are better for learning?'

Q3: Why should children listen to bedtime stories?

Op Without a doubt, listening to bedtime stories is vital, because it profoundly stimulates a young child's Imagination (想象力). Re This is crucial since, hearing complex narratives safely helps kids logically process their own daily emotional struggles. Ex For instance, parents who quietly Hit the books (用功读书) with their toddlers create a highly relaxing routine before sleep.
  • Calms kids down
  • Builds brain power
  • Sleeping much faster
Write your answer (Use O.R.E.):
📉 Band 5 Peer Check: Ask: 'Did your parents read to you when you were little?'
📈 Band 6 Peer Check: Ask: 'Do stories help children understand what is right and wrong?'

Q4: Do Chinese children prefer ancient stories or fairy tales?

Op Admittedly, younger Chinese children strongly prefer magical fairy tales, whereas older students slowly appreciate ancient historical myths. Re The core issue is that, little kids are naturally drawn to extremely colorful, highly Impulsive (冲动的) magical heroes. Ex Conversely (相反地), understanding ancient dynasties actually requires them to successfully Read between the lines (读懂言外之意) to learn deep moral lessons.
  • Depends on age
  • Magic is fun
  • History is harder
Your Bullet Point Notes:
📉 Band 5 Peer Check: Ask: 'Do you prefer stories about dragons or real history?'
📈 Band 6 Peer Check: Ask: 'Does liking history show that a teenager is growing up?'

Q5: Has the internet reduced the attention span required to read long novels?

Op Undeniably, the modern internet has severely ruined our ability to focus, because short videos deliver instant gratification (满足感). Re As a result, a teenager's natural Attention span (注意力持续时间) is completely shattered by constantly scrolling through endless social media feeds. Ex Consequently, reading a huge thick novel now feels incredibly tedious (乏味的) and boring to many young internet users.
  • Yes drastically reduced
  • Short video apps
  • Cannot focus long
Your Bullet Point Notes:
📉 Band 5 Peer Check: Ask: 'Is it hard for you to sit and read for an hour?'
📈 Band 6 Peer Check: Ask: 'Have short internet videos ruined our ability to read long books?'

Q6: Is storytelling an essential part of preserving a culture's history?

Op It is widely acknowledged that, vocal storytelling is absolutely essential, as it successfully guarantees the Preservation (保存) of local heritage. Re This means that, before written languages existed, tribal elders transferred vital survival wisdom through spoken poems. Ex Therefore, verbal traditions beautifully keep ancient societal customs highly relevant and alive for the next generation.
  • Yes very essential
  • Passes down wisdom
  • Grandparents telling tales
Your Bullet Point Notes:
📉 Band 5 Peer Check: Ask: 'How do you learn about your grandparents' lives?'
📈 Band 6 Peer Check: Ask: 'Why are spoken stories sometimes better than history books?'
